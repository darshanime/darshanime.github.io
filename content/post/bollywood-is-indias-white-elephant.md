> **vanity project**: used to describe something that is done with the aim of getting praise, fame, or approval rather than for serious or good reasons


Bollywood is an outlier industry in India. It churns out the highest number of movies anywhere in the world, serving the massive Indian appetite for entertainment. But it's not an outlier for that reason.

In a poor country like India, where human life is not very highly valued and the standard of living is low, the actors live like Gods. 

An average citizen on India earns $1670 per year, while Akshay Kumar earns $35.5 million in 2017. The difference is 21,257x. Note this is against the income of average citizen, not the poorest citizen.

The entertainers live in expansive houses, and are admired and adored by millions across the country. There are instances of temples being created for some actors and they are literally worshiped there. Rajnikant from Tamil Nadu comes to mind. Their victories are celebrated by everyone and their losses are mourned by all. When they come step out, journalists clamor for a shot, running behind them, pleading even. Some wait for many hours to catch a glimpse.

Since the perks are many, there are a lot of folks trying to build sustainable careers in the entertainment industry. Hence, this begs the question, since the supply is in abundance, is it possible to make the system more efficient in terms of not having to pay these individual actors insane amounts of money. Or controlling a larger portion of their lives like signing them to do at least 10 movies for a fixed rate, no matter how big of a star they become etc. The new comers would be willing to sign on even here, since they are being given a break. On the face of it, it seems like a good strategy to control the salary paid out to these actors. 

However, none of that happens. The actors continue to be paid massive sums, their supply is tightly controlled and we have only a few stars at the top.
