In this age of diverse cultures and traditions, morality is commonly defined differently by each segment of the human race. I use segment here as a general abstraction of any groping of humans, could be on religious, ethnic, demographic, social class lines etc. Historically, there have been different cultures, traditions and what may be the norm in a culture might be immoral in the other. Bull fights are common place in Spain, but constitute animal abuse in India. Burqa is a sign of modesty in the East but considered "not ideal" in the west. 

This thinking can lead to an argument that there are no objective moral rules. What ever each culture does is right from their perspective and others have no right to say it is wrong or not. This is a very dangerous idea I think. If there is a tribe on Lakshadweep islands that kills their 1st child to please the gods, we shouldn't have to say this is what their traditions are, it is right for them. We must be able to say this is wrong.

There are objective moral values, even in this world of plurality and diverseness. Sam Harris defines good morals as anything that increases the wellbeing of all human beings. Killing the 1st child is not an ideal way to maximize human existence, not for the parents, surely not for the child.

We need to agree on a baseline of moral values which maximize our collective happiness. Sure, there can be more than 1 right answers, but there are wrong answer too and we must be able to acknowledge them.
