I am very excited to be a part of this year's edition of the Go-Jek engineering bootcamp 004. I had heard before joining that it won't be easy but that I would learn a lot and I was all in for it. 

The bootcamp has various aspects in it, each to build a particular skill or improve understanding in some area. 
We have mandatory reading commitments where we have some of the read the classical books of the industry and then present our summaries before the class. There are code drills where we take a simple problem and try to solve it in the "right" way, exploring ideas about the best practices. We have speaking sessions where we try to present an argument with data and convince others. 

To mentor us are some of the senior engineers at Go-Jek, many of whom have been participants in earlier editions of the bootcamp. This works very well because we all share a lot of context on the common problems that plague us at Go-Jek and how we can solve them going forward. 

One thing that was different from my expectation was that we spend a lot more time talking about soft skills, not just pure technical concepts. I think this is great because we can find the theoretical knowledge elsewhere but this focus on soft skills is rare and difficult to master by oneself.

It has been a great experience so far and I am looking forward to the rest of the bootcamp.
