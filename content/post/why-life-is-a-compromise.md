The human beings are conscious creatures who can experience a wide range of experiences. How wide this spectrum is on the grand scale no one knows. There is no limit to the happiness that it is possible to feel, or the pain it is possible to be in as a human. 

For one, the average Joe spends his life a little on the sad side of the human experience spectrum. There is a lot to be sad about. 

The world is a cruel and lonely place. No one is truly your partner in this journey, you really are alone in the limited time that you have on this planet. People you depend on will betray you, things you wished would turn a certain way won't, your health won't be pink always, your loved ones will one day leave you, and ultimately so will you leave this world. 

In the face of this objectively sad circumstance, what reasons do we have to live, and live happily? There is no perfection anywhere to be seen. If you are a software developer, you will write code that is buggy, which will run on buggy operating systems sitting on hardware that itself is imperfect. Everyone you work with will write just as buggy code as you, and in this cesspit of confused ascii characters, you will toil away your life, bearing grief along the way of a world crumbling around you, to one day die an unknown human the world won't know even existed 5 years later. These ideas aren't new, or my opinion, anyone who spends some time thinking about the human condition would invariably wind up here. 

What makes life worth living then? 

I would argue the fact that this is the same for every single one of us, makes it a little easier to bear. Accepting the fact that this is just how the world is, full of disillusionment, and letting go of idealism, and notions of self importance is what will help bring a little order to the chaos.
