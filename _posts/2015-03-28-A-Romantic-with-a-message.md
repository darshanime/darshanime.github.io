That is true. I have a romantic's heart that finds things to admire everywhere. I feel affection for things ordinary to others. Now before we start giggling and *'Oh, I always thought so...'*s breakout, let us broaden our definition of romance. 

Romance is not just the love between pretty girls and lucky boys. It is more meaningful than that. Romance is the love for the flowers, love for the human bonding, love for the country, love for one's language.

I say I am a romantic to convey that I have a tendency to appreciate small things which people might miss. To me there is a certain beauty in how friends help each other. In how mothers wake up early and prepare food for their kids. There is beauty in how old men remember the days of their prime with a sparkle in their eye and fresh energy in their voice. In how people miss whom they love.

I appreciate all that. Every emotion thrown at me, I absorb. This is isn't always a good thing though for I feel pain equally strongly. I can see the sorrow where others see nothing. I can see the seriousness in my mothers eyes as she bids me goodbye every time the holidays end. She smiles but I feel her sadness.

I see sadness in the withering of flowers. I feel sad when people talk about missed opportunities. About how their lives would have been if only they had grabbed the chance when they could. I see sadness and take in a small part of it.

Above all, I feel the sad terror in the eyes of an animal when it is dragged to the slaughter house, to get butchered. I feel the terror in every muscle of the animal, it's eyes tracking every moment of the sinner with fear, it looking at the blade helpless and paralyzed. Why does this have to be ?

The sight of an animal being killed is not easily forgotten. There is blood everywhere, painful shrieks, twitching of the body, futile leg movements and unimaginable pain before death descends.

It is not just an emotional issue. There is real harm to the environment, oneself and the future of mankind in the act of eating meat.

<br>

________
**_Eating red is harmful for our health._**

It is the cause of many heart diseases. It adds to obesity, diabetes and certain types of cancer. Read the Harvard study [here](http://www.nih.gov/researchmatters/march2012/03262012meat.htm).

_________
**_Eating red is harmful for the environment._**

It takes 7kg of grain to produce 1kg of chicken meat. If people were to eat green, we wouldn't have to produce so much food. There, worlds hunger gone. We wouldn't face water problems. Because we wouldn't need so much water.

__________
**_Eating red harms the ozone layer._** 

The animals release greenhouse gases which adds to global warming. A staggering 51 percent or more of global greenhouse-gas emissions are caused by animal agriculture. [More](http://www.peta.org/issues/animals-used-for-food/global-warming/)

_________
**_Eating red kills innocent animals._**

Animals are entitled to life like us. They feel pain and joy. I feel it is immoral to kill them for food when in fact we have so many other options. Worse, before being killed, they are tortured in crowded and filthy factory farms, many of them so cramped that the inmates can't even turn around freely. There is so much sadness in this fact. Read Rolling Stone talk about this [here](http://www.rollingstone.com/feature/belly-beast-meat-factory-farms-animal-activists). More by [PETA](http://www.peta.org/issues/animals-used-for-food/factory-farming/).

_____________
<br>

I understand this is a personal choice and people don't want blogs to tell them what to eat. But the idea of vegetarianism deserves more attention than it is given. Humans are biologically vegetarian. Our teeth are like those of herbivores. Our closest relatives on the biological tree are all vegetarian. We have long intestines to digest cellulose better. We sip water like herbivores and not lick like carnivores.

We humans are gifted with intellect. We are rational, or so we believe. Shouldn't this be an open shut case then ? 
Why are we so off the mark in our decision here ?

We will all die one day. After we are gone, few things will matter. While we are alive, let us live fair to our fellow beings. Life is good when everyone is living it with joy. Even animals. Peace.
