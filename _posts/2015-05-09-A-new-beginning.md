<br><br>
**What is this entry about ?**

I love open source. The very idea of open, naked code for all to learn from and use for free is amazing. Add to it social coding. Expert developers guide you in your contributions to the project. This is where you get to meet PhDs and get your work reviewed by them.

I had heard about [Google Summer of Code](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Summer_of_Code) program. It is this magical program where Google sets you up with an organization working with open source software for the summer and also pays you good stipend. I decided to try for it.

I worked for [SymPy](http://www.sympy.org/en/index.html) and [PyDy](https://pydy.org) for a few months. Spoiler; I did <span style="color:red">not</span> succeed. I can make excuses about Google cutting accepted projects from 1300s in 2014 to 1000s in 2015. I can say the organization of my alliance did not get chosen independently. However, the truth is I have been a lousy programmer. I wrote shabby code. I did little work and talked too much. This series is about changing this attitude.

I plan to do this :

  * Write about what I learned in my stint with SymPy and PyDy. <br>
  * Talk about my contributions to the projects.<br>
  * Talk about the amazing people I met. <br>

Next, I plan to write about my experiences with the project I wish to work with now : [**scikit-learn**](http://scikit-learn.org/stable/index.html).<br>
I shall document each engagement with the project, each issue I help resolve, each PR I issue. It will read like a diary.

<br>
**Why are you doing this ?**

I have my reasons.

This will serve as a guide for anyone who would like to embark on this noble path of contributing to open source. I will talk about 'How I setup my environment' to 'How I issued my first Pull Request'. I will link some interesting articles, guides, hacks I come across along the way. The series should trace my learning curve.
<br><br>

There is a selfish reason too. I am doing this for myself.<br>

As a student, I learn best when I make notes, when I underline what is important, when I am not lousy. In the process of writing this series, I will research, write cleaner code, structure my ideas in a better way. All this will teach me too.

So, excited ?
